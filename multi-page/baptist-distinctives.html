B – Biblical Authority

As Baptists we believe that the Bible is the sole authority that governs the church.

  • We believe that all Scripture is given by inspiration of God and is not given to our own interpretation.(2 Timothy 3:16; 2 Peter 1:20-21)
  • We believe that the very words of the Bible are God’s words.(Matthew 4:4; Matthew 5:18; Matthew 24:35; Exodus 24:4a; 2 Samuel 23:2)
  • We believe that the Authorized King James Version is God’spreserved translation given to the English speaking people.(Psalm 12:6-7; Psalm 33:11; Psalm 119:89; 2 Timothy 3:15-16)
  • We believe that all parts of the Bible are equally inspired.(2 Timothy 3:16)
  • We believe that the Bible is inerrant (not liable to be proven false or mistaken).(Titus 1:2)
  • We believe that the Bible is infallible (the Bible is without error in its teaching).(John 17:17; Psalm 119:160)

 

A – Autonomy of the Local Church

As Baptists we believe that each individual church governs itself and is not subject to a denominational hierarchy. The proper method of church government is congregational.

  • The local Church has Christ as its head(Theocracy).(Colossians 1:18)
  • The local Church has the pastor as an under-shepherd.(1 Peter 5:2-4)
  • The local Church decides all issues.(Matthew 18:17)
  • The local Church chooses their own Bishops (Pastors).(Acts 1:15-26)
  • The local Church chooses their own Deacons.(Acts 6:1-5)
  • The local Church sends out missionaries.(Acts 13:1-3)
  • The local Church disciplines its members.(1 Corinthians 5)

 

P – Priesthood of the Believer

As Baptists we believe that each person that trusts in Christ as their own personal Saviour has the same access to the throne of grace as any other believer. There is no human being between us and the Great High Priest, Jesus Christ.

  • As Christians (Priests) we are sacred to God.(1 Corinthians 6:20)
  • As Christians(Priests) we are set apart for the Lord’s work.(1 John 2:27)
  • As Christians(Priests) we have the privilege to pray for others.(1 Timothy 2:1-3)
  • As Christians(Priests) we are to reconcile men to God.(2 Corinthians 5:20)
  • As Christians(Priests) we can all go to the Bible for spiritual guidance and seek the Holy Spirit’s instruction to understand the Bible. (John 5:39; John 16:13)
  • As Christians(Priests) we can go directly to God and confess our sins and expect God to forgive us of our sins. (1 John 1:9)
  • As Christians(Priests), if you are living in obedience, our prayers can be just as effective as any pastor. (Psalm 66:18; James 5:16)

 

T – Two Ordinances(Baptism and Lord’s Supper)

As Baptists we believe in two ordinances. We believe that Baptism and the Lord’s Supper are the only two ordinances practiced by the New Testament Church. These are the only two ordinances within the Bible that are 1) instituted by Jesus Christ in the Gospels, 2) practiced by the early church in the book of Acts, and 3)instructed upon in the Epistles.
Baptism

  • We believe that the act of baptism is by immersion only. (Matthew 3:6, 16; John 3:23; Acts 8:38-39)
  • We believe that the picture of baptism requires immersion. (Romans 6:4-5; Colossians 2:12)
  • We believe we identify with a body of doctrine (Christ) through baptism. (Matthew 3:13-15; Mark 1:4, 15; John 1:26-27)
  • We believe that through baptism we identify with the local church and that church’s doctrine. (Acts 2:41-42)

Lord’s Supper(Communion)
  • We believe that the Lord’s Supper is an act of obedience. (1 Corinthians 11:24-25)
  • We believe that the Lord’s Supper is an act of remembrance. (1 Corinthians 11:24-25)
  • We believe that the Lord’s Supper is an act of praise and thanksgiving. (1 Corinthians 11:24)
  • We believe that the Lord’s Supper is an act of testimony. (1 Corinthians 11:26)
  • We believe that the Lord’s Supper is an act of examination. (1 Corinthians 11:28)
  • We believe that the Lord’s Supper is an act of communion. (1 Corinthians 10:16-17)

 

I – Individual Soul Liberty

We believe that each person has the right to believe the way his conscience dictates. It is not within a person’s rights to force a belief system upon another person. No person is accountable to any other person for his/her personal belief system, but each person is accountable before God for his/her own beliefs. We also believe that Individual Soul Liberty is limited to the differences between likes and dislikes and does not enter the realm of right and wrong.(Romans 14:5-12)

 

S – Saved and Baptized Church Membership

We believe that only those who have placed saving faith in Jesus Christ, and then have submitted to baptism by immersion after salvation, under the proper authority, meet the requirements for church membership.(Acts 2:41; 1 Corinthians 1:2, 13-16; Matthew 28:18-20)

 

T – Two Offices (Pastor and Deacon)

We believe that there are only two biblical offices within the church. These offices are Pastor and Deacon.
Pastor

  • We believe that God has given pastors certain responsibilities within the church which include feeding the flock of God and taking the oversight of the flock of God with the right heart.(1 Peter 5:1-4)
  • We believe that the pastor of the church must preach the Word of Truth accurately and without apology.(Acts 20:17-28)
  • We believe that the pastor must live accordingly to certain qualifications and must be qualified in all areas to fulfill the office.(1 Timothy 3:1-7; Titus 1:6-9)
  • We believe that pastors are a gift from God that are given to the church to bring members into spiritual maturity.(Ephesians 4:11-16)

Deacon
  • The deacon is a servant of the church that ministers to the needs of the members.
  • We believe a man must have certain qualifications before being able to fill the office of a deacon.(1 Timothy 3:8-13)
  • We believe that the deacon is called to minister to the people as a help to the pastor which allows the pastor to devote his time to study.(Acts 6:1-4)

 

S – Separation of Church and State

As Baptist we believe that there is a biblical responsibility of separation of church and state.

  • We believe that Jesus taught separation of church and state.(Matthew 22:15-22)
  • We believe there are Old Testament examples of separation of church and state. Moses was in government and Aaron was a priest.
  • We believe that the church is designed to influence the government, but should not run the government.
  • We understand that the term “separation of church and state” is not found in our governments founding documents.
  • We believe that the United States of America was founded upon biblical principles which are clearly uncovered in the history of America. Consider the Rhode Island Charter as an example.
  • We believe that the United States of America was founded upon biblical principles which are clearly stated in the Bible. Consider the three offices in government: Judicial, Legislative, and Executive. These three areas of government are found as examples in Isaiah 33:22.
